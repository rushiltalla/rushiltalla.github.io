Hot Chocolate Cookies

Servings: 6 cookies Time: 60 minutes

Equipment

  • Baking Tray

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 2 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/4 cup chocolate syrup
  • 1.5 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup hot chocolate powder
  • 1 cup chocolate chips

Directions

  1. In a medium sized bowl, mix the flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, and hot chocolate powder.
  2. In a different bowl, cream together the softened butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar. It should form a combined slurry.
  3. Beat the egg, vanilla, and chocolate syrup into the butter-sugar mixture.
  4. Mix the wet and dry ingredients until combined.
  5. Add chocolate chips and mix well.
  6. Roll the dough into 6 balls (or more for smaller cookies) and place on a plate. Press the balls slightly so they look more like disks
  7. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (helps keep their shape).
  8. Preheat oven to 375Â°F. Line the baking tray with parchment paper.
  9. Remove dough from fridge and move to the baking tray.
  10. Bake in the oven for about 10 minutes, poke center of a cookie with a toothpick/fork test. The fork should be mostly clean (ignore chocolate); take the cookies out before they are fully cooked as they continue to cook as they cool.
  11. Let them sit on the baking tray for 8 minutes before moving to a cooling rack.
