Apple Cinnamon Muffins

Servings: 12 servings Time: 60 minutes

Equipment

  • 12 count muffin pan

Ingredients

  • Topping:
  • 1/3 cup light brown sugar
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter (melted)
  • 1/3 cup whole wheat flour
  • 2/3 cup rolled oats
  • Muffin:
  • 1 cups whole wheat flour
  • 0.75 cup all purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup unslated butter (softened)
  • 0.75 cup granulated/light brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1.5 cups peeled and chopped apples (~<2 medium apples, dice to about 1-0.5 inch pieces)

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F.
  2. Crumb topping - mix the dry ingredients first, then add the melted butter. Try to keep large clumps from breaking apart (do not over mix). Set asside.
  3. Whisk the flour, baking soda, baking powder, cinnamon, and salt together in a large bowl.
  4. Cream the sugar and butter together. Add eggs, yogurt, and vanilla extract to the sugar and butter mixture. Mix well until creamed together (might look a bit curdled)
  5. Combine dry and wet in one large bowl, and mix until no flour pockets remain. Fold in the apples.
  6. Evenly distribute the batter into the muffin trays (can use liner, or grease). Gently add and press the crumb topping to each muffin.
  7. Bake for 5 minutes at 425°F, then bring down to 350°F for about 15-18 minutes (test with toothpick/fork).
  8. Let rest for 5 minutes, then remove from baking tray
